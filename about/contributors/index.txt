9:T715,<h2 id="primary-author"><a class="no-underline" href="#primary-author">Primary Author</a></h2>
<p><strong>Derald R Grimwood Jr.</strong> is the originator and primary author of the x64base, DotTalk++, and LabTalk concept line. The project preserves the xBase lineage while using modern C++ implementation work, self-documenting contracts, and an education-first laboratory model.</p>
<h2 id="contributors"><a class="no-underline" href="#contributors">Contributors</a></h2>
<p><strong>Nathaniel Grimwood, IT Director</strong> is recognized as a human contributor who provided AI-related advice and guidance to Derald R Grimwood Jr. during the LabTalk/x64base development work.</p>
<p>The contributor model for this project treats AI-assisted development as part of the engineering record. Human contributors may advise on AI use, source organization, implementation planning, documentation, website publication, and how source contracts connect to public-facing explanations. Human review remains the authority for release decisions, authorship, licensing, and trademark use.</p>
<h2 id="contribution-record"><a class="no-underline" href="#contribution-record">Contribution Record</a></h2>
<p>LabTalk is being developed as a living laboratory campus. Contributions are expected to leave a trail through one or more of these surfaces:</p>
<ul>
<li>Source code and build scripts.</li>
<li><code>@dottalk.usage</code>, <code>@dottalk.contract</code>, and related SelfDoc contracts.</li>
<li>Case stories and runtime proofs.</li>
<li>Website pages generated from the vertical documentation system.</li>
<li>Lab exercises, education features, and GUI/portal experiments.</li>
</ul>
