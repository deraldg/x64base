15:Tcd9,<h2 id="mission"><a class="no-underline" href="#mission">Mission</a></h2>
<p>To democratize database literacy by providing clear, stable, transparent tools that teach as much as they perform.</p>
<h2 id="vision"><a class="no-underline" href="#vision">Vision</a></h2>
<p>A world where students, developers, and communities understand data structures, file formats, and relational thinking
through approachable, historically grounded tools.</p>
<h2 id="core-identity"><a class="no-underline" href="#core-identity">Core Identity</a></h2>
<p>x64base begins with the database engine. The DBF runtime, 64-bit table work,
memo/index architecture, work areas, relations, buffering, locking, validation,
and data-mutation behavior are the technical center of the project.</p>
<p>The outside-visible thesis is broader than "64-bit xBase": x64base is a
database system that uses its own metadata and documentation infrastructure to
describe, validate, and increasingly prove itself.</p>
<p>Around that core, DotTalk++, SelfDoc, the Master Documentation Organizer, and
the Laboratory Campus form the teaching and analysis system. They exist to
explain the engine, test it, document it, preserve its history, and make its
behavior teachable. They should not be described as replacing the database
engine; they are the system that helps the engine become visible, reviewable,
and learnable.</p>
<p>SelfDoc and MDO also record the way the project is actually being built. They
began as cooperating project-documentation conversations and are now becoming
part of the system itself: a repeatable path from source, HELP, comments,
contracts, proofs, and review into manuals, diagrams, curriculum, and public
website sections.</p>
<h2 id="academic-collaboration-direction"><a class="no-underline" href="#academic-collaboration-direction">Academic Collaboration Direction</a></h2>
<p>x64base is under active development. Some visible gaps are not abandoned features; they are lanes that have not yet reached the right proof, documentation, or curriculum shape for public release.</p>
<p>The next phase is to open the project to academic interest and outside review. Desired input includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>computer science professors who can evaluate current theory, algorithms, data structures, runtime design, and systems-programming lessons.</li>
<li>database professors and practitioners who can review DBF lineage, relational modeling, indexing, locking, triggers, table buffering, and data-mutation lessons.</li>
<li>technical writing teachers who can advise on documentation standards, student-facing explanations, manual structure, terminology, and proof-backed publication.</li>
<li>general education specialists who can help shape the Laboratory Campus as a learning environment instead of only a technical reference shelf.</li>
<li>researchers and instructors from different schools of learning who can help test whether examples, labs, diagrams, and assessments support more than one learning style.</li>
</ul>
