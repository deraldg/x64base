<h2 id="lmdb-credit-in-the-cdx-system"><a class="no-underline" href="#lmdb-credit-in-the-cdx-system">LMDB Credit in the CDX System</a></h2>
<p>LMDB deserves explicit credit in the x64base story because it gives the CDX indexing lane a durable, high-performance embedded backend. The x64base design keeps two ideas separate:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>CDX is the logical container</strong> shown to users, students, and commands.</li>
<li><strong>LMDB is the physical backend</strong> used to store and traverse built index data.</li>
</ul>
<p>That separation lets LabTalk teach database indexing at two levels. Beginners can learn records, logical order, tags, <code>SET ORDER</code>, and <code>SEEK</code> without needing backend vocabulary first. Advanced students can open the implementation layer and see how a memory-mapped key-value database can support ordered navigation, rebuild workflows, and index diagnostics.</p>
