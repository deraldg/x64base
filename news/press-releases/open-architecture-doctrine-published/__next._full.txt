14:T421,<p>The current publication pass makes the x64base architecture story explicit instead of leaving it implied.</p>
<p>This update aligns the public narrative around several active seams:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>INX</code>, <code>CNX</code>, <code>CDX</code>, and <code>LMDB</code> as distinct indexing families with different roles</li>
<li><code>SCX</code> and <code>SIX</code> as educational and lab-facing index experiments</li>
<li>CLI, TUI, ERSATZ, wx, and Python workbench lanes as consumers of runtime truth</li>
<li>command, function, trigger, polling, and documentation seams as intentional extension points</li>
</ul>
<p>The practical rule remains simple:</p>
<ol>
<li>the engine owns database truth</li>
<li>front ends and browsers consume that truth</li>
<li>HELP, metadata, SelfDoc, manuals, and the site describe and validate it</li>
</ol>
<p>This release is not a branding rewrite.
