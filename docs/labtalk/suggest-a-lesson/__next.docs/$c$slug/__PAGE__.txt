6:Tca9,<p>The Laboratory Campus is open to lesson suggestions and review. Useful input can
come from students, teachers, CS faculty, database practitioners, technical
writers, curriculum reviewers, non-profit partners, and experienced developers.</p>
<p>For now, the intake path is static and email-based. A writable form can come
later after the project chooses durable storage and moderation rules.</p>
<h2 id="what-to-suggest"><a class="no-underline" href="#what-to-suggest">What to Suggest</a></h2>
<p>Good suggestions fit one of these lanes:</p>





























<table><thead><tr><th>Lane</th><th>Examples</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Student lesson</td><td>A lab on records, indexes, relations, DotScript, mutation safety, HELP/CMDHELP, or database history.</td></tr><tr><td>Career lesson</td><td>A lesson learned while building, recovering, documenting, testing, or teaching systems.</td></tr><tr><td>Review request</td><td>Feedback on prerequisites, learning sequence, accessibility, proof labels, or classroom safety.</td></tr><tr><td>Proof gap</td><td>A place where the site claims something but needs stronger evidence or clearer status.</td></tr><tr><td>Dataset or case idea</td><td>A small inspectable dataset, historical case, or classroom scenario that could support a lesson.</td></tr></tbody></table>
<h2 id="suggestion-template"><a class="no-underline" href="#suggestion-template">Suggestion Template</a></h2>
<p>Use this structure:</p>
<pre><code class="language-text">Title:
Suggested lane: student lesson | career lesson | review | proof gap | dataset/case
Audience:
Concepts:
Relevant page or file:
What the learner should do:
Expected observation:
What proof exists:
What still needs review:
Safety or mutation risk:
Your role or background:
</code></pre>
<h2 id="email-intake"><a class="no-underline" href="#email-intake">Email Intake</a></h2>
<p>Send suggestions to <a href="mailto:deraldg@msn.com?subject=LabTalk%20lesson%20suggestion">deraldg@msn.com</a>.</p>
<p>Include "LabTalk lesson suggestion" in the subject and paste the template into
the message body. For academic review, mention whether your perspective is CS,
databases, technical writing, curriculum, general education, learning theory, or
industry practice.</p>
<h2 id="review-rules"><a class="no-underline" href="#review-rules">Review Rules</a></h2>
<p>Lesson suggestions are strongest when they preserve these boundaries:</p>
<ul>
<li>live runtime proof is different from source-defined behavior.</li>
<li>source memory is different from reviewed historical fact.</li>
<li>a simulation can be useful, but it must be labeled.</li>
<li>student-ready material needs setup, safety notes, expected observations, and proof links.</li>
<li>a draft can be public when the status is honest.</li>
</ul>
