15:T16c4,<p>LabTalk uses the database evolution path to teach why records, reports, files,
schemas, transactions, and proof still matter in the AI era.</p>
<p>The source teaching artifact is the LabTalk / DotTalk++ systems storyboard deck:</p>
<ul>
<li>Source artifact: <code>D:\code\ccode\docs\media\LabTalk_DotTalkpp_Systems_Storyboard_Deck.pptx</code></li>

</ul>
<h2 id="teaching-thesis"><a class="no-underline" href="#teaching-thesis">Teaching Thesis</a></h2>
<p>Students who understand data, records, reports, and systems are better prepared
to work with AI. LabTalk should make that path visible as a living laboratory,
not only as a history lecture.</p>
<h2 id="evolution-spine"><a class="no-underline" href="#evolution-spine">Evolution Spine</a></h2>























































<table><thead><tr><th>Era</th><th>LabTalk Framing</th><th>What Students Should See</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Punch cards and batch files</td><td>Data as physical records and scheduled processing</td><td>The cost of layout, order, validation, and correction</td></tr><tr><td>COBOL business systems</td><td>Records, fields, reports, and institutional workflows</td><td>Business rules encoded as repeatable processing</td></tr><tr><td>ARPANET and connected computing</td><td>Systems begin sharing information across networks</td><td>Data becomes part of communication infrastructure</td></tr><tr><td>JUMPS and mainframe case systems</td><td>Personnel, pay, status, and audit consequences</td><td>Database errors affect real people and operations</td></tr><tr><td>CODASYL and owner-member data</td><td>Enterprise relationships before relational SQL dominance</td><td>Databases model real operational relationships, not just storage</td></tr><tr><td>xBase and desktop database tools</td><td>Working programmers build useful apps from tables, indexes, and screens</td><td>Accessibility, productivity, and local ownership of data systems</td></tr><tr><td>Microsoft data pipelines</td><td>Access, Excel, ODBC, SQL Server, and office workflows</td><td>Interoperability becomes a practical bridge between users and systems</td></tr><tr><td>ERP, imaging, auto-ID, and industrial data</td><td>Transactions connect documents, products, shipping, invoicing, and process data</td><td>Data becomes the connective tissue of the organization</td></tr><tr><td>Cloud computing and AI</td><td>Distributed services, large datasets, model-assisted work, and governance</td><td>AI literacy depends on knowing what the data means and how it was produced</td></tr></tbody></table>
<h2 id="deck-storyboard"><a class="no-underline" href="#deck-storyboard">Deck Storyboard</a></h2>
<p>The current deck organizes the path as a classroom sequence:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Foundations: COBOL and connected computers</strong> - business data, records, and ARPANET as preparation for AI literacy.</li>
<li><strong>Case study: JUMPS Army system</strong> - institutional scale, traceability, auditability, and consequences of error.</li>
<li><strong>Unisys / CODASYL COBOL at Alcoa</strong> - owner-member sets and enterprise relationships.</li>
<li><strong>xBase as a major platform</strong> - dBASE, Clipper, FoxPro, Visual FoxPro, and Microsoft interoperability.</li>
<li><strong>Earthkids to CAREPAX</strong> - daycare administration, receivables, vaccination scheduling, and market-fit lessons.</li>
<li><strong>Digital transfer, ERP, and industrial scale</strong> - TitleSCAN, document imaging, SQL workflows, auto-ID, transactions, and semiconductor process data.</li>
<li><strong>DotTalk++ / LabTalk and the AI future</strong> - the xBase future that did not happen, revived as a learning lab for explainable data systems.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="lab-implementation-pattern"><a class="no-underline" href="#lab-implementation-pattern">Lab Implementation Pattern</a></h2>
<p>Every era should become a small repeatable lab:</p>
<pre><code class="language-text">historical concept -> source artifact -> dataset -> command or UI -> proof transcript -> lesson
</code></pre>
<p>This lets LabTalk simulate or demonstrate a horizontal slice through database
history. Some slices can be live. Others can be carefully simulated when the
original hardware, software, or data is unavailable.</p>
