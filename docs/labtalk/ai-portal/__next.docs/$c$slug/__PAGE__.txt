6:T1069,<h2 id="mandatory-status"><a class="no-underline" href="#mandatory-status">Mandatory Status</a></h2>
<p>The AI Portal lane is <strong>Alpha/Experimental</strong>.</p>
<p>It must not be described as production autonomous memory, an independent source
of project truth, or a safety boundary that can replace source, runtime, HELP,
metadata, contracts, review, or human authorization.</p>
<h2 id="purpose"><a class="no-underline" href="#purpose">Purpose</a></h2>
<p>The AI Portal is the AI-facing entrance to the Laboratory Campus. It is being
designed to prepare an AI for a specific series of project tasks without
requiring the model to ingest the entire ecosystem or depend on lost chat
history.</p>
<p>Its intended path is:</p>
<pre><code class="language-text">task
-> owning project and lane
-> curated, typed jumps between artifacts
-> authority, freshness, risk, and proof checks
-> bounded task context packet
-> guarded action plan
-> proof readback and durable closeout
</code></pre>
<p>The working metaphor is <strong>frontal memory</strong>. The implementation must remain
inspectable: every included artifact should identify why it was selected, what
authority it has, what evidence supports it, and what remains missing.</p>
<h2 id="what-exists-today"><a class="no-underline" href="#what-exists-today">What Exists Today</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>repo-local AI orientation and assimilation documents;</li>
<li>LabTalk project, app, lesson, concept, proof, and portal registries;</li>
<li>a local registry browser and launcher;</li>
<li>read-only <code>MAINT AI</code> orientation commands;</li>
<li>runtime transcripts and truth-audit reports;</li>
<li>the APH-0 through APH-6 hardening plan.</li>
</ul>
<p>These pieces are useful but do not yet constitute validated autonomous project
memory.</p>
<h2 id="hardening-gates"><a class="no-underline" href="#hardening-gates">Hardening Gates</a></h2>





































<table><thead><tr><th>Gate</th><th>Outcome</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>APH-0</td><td>Preserve the lane, remove unexplained drift, make startup reproducible, and establish tests.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-1</td><td>Turn flat registry metadata into a validated graph of typed, directional synapses.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-2</td><td>Compile deterministic, bounded, read-only task context packets—the first useful MVP.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-3</td><td>Add reviewed capabilities, risk classes, approvals, containment, cancellation, and proof transcripts.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-4</td><td>Make task preparation and jump explanation the primary portal experience.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-5</td><td>Curate the graph and packet history in x64base with deterministic portable exports and recovery.</td></tr><tr><td>APH-6</td><td>Turn portal behavior into DotTalk++ lessons and measure cold-start task performance.</td></tr></tbody></table>
<h2 id="safety-and-authority"><a class="no-underline" href="#safety-and-authority">Safety and Authority</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>Read-only preparation is the default.</li>
<li>A registry record cannot grant itself execution authority.</li>
<li>Missing or contradictory evidence must be visible before action.</li>
<li>Mutating operations require explicit, separately reviewed capabilities and
approval.</li>
<li>A generated packet is working context, not a new source of runtime truth.</li>
<li>Website summaries remain downstream reviewed derivatives.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="campus-role"><a class="no-underline" href="#campus-role">Campus Role</a></h2>
<p>The AI Portal is both an engineering tool and a lesson. Its graph records,
context budgets, validation failures, safety gates, transcripts, and evaluation
results should become inspectable DotTalk++ and Laboratory Campus material.</p>
<p>This makes AI participation part of the same co-development loop as engine,
documentation, proof, and curriculum work—without pretending the experimental
portal is already complete.</p>
