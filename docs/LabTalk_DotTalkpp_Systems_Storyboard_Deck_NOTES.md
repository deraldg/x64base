# LabTalk / DotTalk++ Systems Storyboard Deck Notes

Source deck: `LabTalk_DotTalkpp_Systems_Storyboard_Deck.pptx`

## Slide 1

Opening framing: this deck is independent of the DotTalk++ executable. It is a LabTalk / SelfDoc teaching artifact that can be used as a standalone classroom deck or later expanded into a separate manual. The teaching goal is to connect database history to AI literacy.

## Slide 2

Page 1: Start with foundations. COBOL made business work expressible to computers. Business records became fields, records, and reports. ARPANET introduced connected computing. The teaching point is that understanding how computers organize information is preparation for understanding AI systems.

## Slide 3

Page 2: Use JUMPS as the first case study. Emphasize institutional scale, rules, traceability, auditability, and consequences of error. Data management is not abstract; it affects pay, status, trust, and operations.

## Slide 4

Page 3: Show that databases are not merely storage. CODASYL-style owner-member sets modeled real enterprise relationships. Industrial data connects customers, orders, materials, shipping, invoicing, and reporting.

## Slide 5

Page 4: Treat xBase as a major platform, not a footnote. dBASE, Clipper, FoxPro, and Visual FoxPro enabled working programmers to build real database applications. Microsoft data pipelines around Access, Excel, ODBC, and SQL Server become the bridge to modern interoperability.

## Slide 6

Page 5: Corrected Earthkids / CAREPAX framing. This is daycare administration, receivables, and vaccination scheduling, not environmental education. The teaching lesson is market fit: a useful system can fail if users lack money, support capacity, or technical readiness.

## Slide 7

Page 6: Move from TitleSCAN and document imaging through ERP, SQL workflows, auto-ID, transaction capture, and semiconductor process data. As systems grow, data becomes connective tissue.

## Slide 8

Page 7: Close with the central thesis: DotTalk++ tells the story of the xBase future that did not happen. It is not nostalgia alone; it preserves, inspects, explains, and interoperates across data systems. Students who understand records, reports, and systems are better positioned to work with AI.
