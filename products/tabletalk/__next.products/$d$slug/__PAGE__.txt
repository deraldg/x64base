2:T46d,<h2 id="overview"><a class="no-underline" href="#overview">Overview</a></h2>
<p>TableTalk is the product/lane name for table buffering in DotTalk++.</p>
<p>Its subject is not general table manipulation or table browsing. Its subject is
the buffered editing state around open DBF areas: ON/OFF buffering, DIRTY versus
clean state, STALE versus fresh fields, buffered row changes, and the
<code>COMMIT</code>/<code>ROLLBACK</code> lifecycle.</p>
<p>Relevant runtime areas include:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>SET TABLE BUFFER ON|OFF [ALL]</code>.</li>
<li><code>TABLE</code> and <code>TABLE BUFFER STATUS</code> reporting.</li>
<li><code>TABLE BUFFER DUMP</code> for buffered change inspection.</li>
<li><code>REPLACE</code> and <code>CALCWRITE</code> when buffering is ON.</li>
<li><code>COMMIT</code> and <code>ROLLBACK</code>.</li>
<li>dirty/stale field evidence such as <code>DIRTY | STALE [DOB]</code>.</li>
</ul>
