12:T562,<h2 id="overview"><a class="no-underline" href="#overview">Overview</a></h2>
<p>TupTalk is best described today as the tuple-facing part of the DotTalk++
inspection model, not a separately finished standalone product.</p>
<p>Its subject is the row as a tuple: current record display, projected row output,
tuple export, tuple validation, and relation-derived tuple grids.</p>
<p>The local command surface includes tuple and browser-related commands such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>SIMPLEBROWSE</code> / <code>SB</code></li>
<li><code>SMARTBROWSE</code> / <code>SM</code> / <code>SMART</code></li>
<li><code>SMARTLIST</code></li>
<li><code>TUPLE</code></li>
<li><code>TUPTALK</code></li>
<li><code>TUPEXPORT</code></li>
<li><code>TUPVALIDATE</code></li>
<li>relation-aware <code>ERSATZ</code> grid output</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="typical-tasks"><a class="no-underline" href="#typical-tasks">Typical tasks</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>Inspect current rows as tuples instead of raw table internals.</li>
<li>Review projected tuple-style rows.</li>
<li>Explore relation-derived row output.</li>
<li>Validate tuple/export behavior while keeping table mutation separate.</li>
</ul>
