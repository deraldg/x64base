Status: draft
Audience: developer, educator, maintainer
Registry ID: lesson.career.legacy_systems_as_labs

## Career Lesson

Legacy systems can become strong learning labs when their old constraints are
made visible instead of hidden.

For LabTalk, this means DBF files, indexes, fixed records, command shells,
metadata, and source recovery are not just old implementation details. They are
teaching surfaces.

The LabTalk / DotTalk++ systems storyboard deck also belongs here as a
resume-style career artifact. It records the lived path behind the technical
lesson, not only a classroom sequence.

## LabTalk Reading

The Laboratory Campus can use legacy material in three ways:

- as live runtime evidence when the current system runs it.
- as historical case material when the story is reviewed.
- as simulation when live behavior is intentionally reconstructed.

## Student Use

This can become a student lesson after the source-memory boundary is explicit.
Students should know what is live, what is remembered, what is reconstructed,
and what still needs historical review.
