- Early xBase tooling and teaching materials
- First experiments with inspectable file formats

## 2000s — Practical iteration

- Field-tested workflows
- Refinement of indexing and memo handling concepts

## 2010s — Language & tooling

- Scripting approaches aimed at education and repeatable data work
- CLI-first utilities for inspection and maintenance

## 2026 — Canonical unification

- DBF_64 and FPT64 formalized
- Documentation, licensing, curriculum, and tooling consolidated under a single platform
- SelfDoc and the Master Documentation Organizer become part of the system architecture: documentation, proof, and artifact-protection lanes created to keep AI-assisted work reviewable and prevent damage to source, HELP data, generated manuals, diagrams, and staged publication artifacts.
